# Link-Building & PR Outreach Targets

> Goal: Get backlinks from health, parenting, and food safety sites
> to boost domain authority for dye-free content cluster.
> All outreach must be genuine value-add — no paid links, no spam.

---

## TIER 1: High-Authority Health & Food Safety Sites

These sites cover food safety news and would potentially cover the dye-free movement or link to our educational content as a resource.

| Target | Type | Angle | Contact |
|--------|------|-------|---------|
| EWG.org (Environmental Working Group) | Nonprofit | Their Food Scores database tracks dyes — pitch our ingredient transparency as a case study | editorial@ewg.org or media page |
| Center for Science in the Public Interest (CSPI) | Nonprofit | They literally petitioned the FDA to ban dyes — pitch our blog posts as a resource they can link to | media@cspinet.org |
| ConsumerReports.org | Media | They've covered the FDA dye debate — pitch our Red 40 / Yellow 5 content as supplementary resources | tips@consumerreports.org |
| FoodSafetyNews.com | Trade media | Covers FDA regulatory actions — pitch the dye ban timeline content | publisher@foodsafetynews.com |
| Food & Environment Reporting Network (FERN) | Investigative journalism | Covers food system issues — pitch the EU vs US double standard story | tips@thefern.org |

**Pitch angle for Tier 1:** "We created an educational content series on artificial food dyes for consumers — covering the EU/US regulatory gap, specific dye research, and practical label-reading guides. Happy to share as a resource if you're covering this topic."

## RULES

1. **Never pay for links.** Google penalizes paid backlinks.
2. **Never mass-email.** Personalize every outreach.
3. **Always offer value first** — educational content, free product, a story angle.
4. **Follow up once** after 7 days. If no response after follow-up, move on.
5. **Don't beg.** If they're not interested, respect it.
6. **Track everything** in the table above.
