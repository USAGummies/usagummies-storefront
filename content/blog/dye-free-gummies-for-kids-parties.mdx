## What "dye-free" typically means

In candy, dye-free usually means no synthetic color additives such as FD&C colors. Instead, color can come from fruit or vegetable concentrates, spices, or natural color sources. Labels vary, so the best approach is to read the ingredient list and look for clear statements like "no artificial colors."

## How to build a dye-free party snack table

A kid-friendly table can still be colorful without artificial dyes. Here is a simple framework you can use.

- Choose a main gummy option in bright, naturally colored shades.
- Add fruit cups or fresh fruit skewers for extra color.
- Include crunchy items like popcorn, pretzels, or baked chips.
- Offer a simple dip, like yogurt or a light frosting cup.

## Pick flavors and textures that kids love

Dye-free gummies should still taste great. When shopping, look for:

- A mix of fruit-forward flavors, so kids can pick favorites.
- A chewy texture that holds up on a snack table.
- Portion-friendly sizes for easy serving.

## A quick party planning checklist

Keep planning simple with this list.

- Decide your color theme early.
- Confirm how many guests you will serve.
- Pick one primary gummy option and two supporting snacks.
- Prepare serving bowls and tongs to keep the table tidy.
- Check ingredients for any known sensitivities.

## FAQs

### Are dye-free gummies always labeled "dye-free"?

Not always. Some labels say "no artificial colors" or list natural color sources. The ingredient list is the most reliable place to check.

### Do dye-free gummies still look colorful?

Yes. Natural colors can still be vibrant, especially when paired with fruit or other colorful snacks.

### How many gummies should I plan per child?

A small handful per child is usually enough when combined with other snacks. Plan a bit extra for refills.

### What else goes well with dye-free gummies at parties?

Fruit skewers, popcorn, yogurt cups, and pretzels all pair well and keep the table balanced.

Dye-free gummies make it easy to serve a fun, kid-friendly treat without artificial dyes. Start with the ingredient list, build your color theme, and keep portions simple.
