Gummy bears are the one candy that works everywhere. Kids' birthday parties, wedding favors, office candy bowls, holiday gift bags, 4th of July cookouts — there's never a wrong occasion for gummy bears.

But figuring out *how much* to buy, *how to display* them, and *which ones to choose* when allergies and dietary concerns are involved? That takes a little planning. Here's everything you need to know.

## How Many Gummy Bears Do You Need?

This is the first question everyone asks, and the answer depends on how you're serving them.

### Candy Table / Bowl (Self-Serve)
- **Rule of thumb:** 1/4 pound per guest
- **For 25 guests:** about 6-7 bags (7.5 oz each)
- **For 50 guests:** about 12-14 bags
- **For 100 guests:** about 25 bags

People take more than you'd expect from a self-serve table, especially kids. When in doubt, round up. Leftover gummy bears are never a problem.

### Party Favor Bags (Pre-Portioned)
- **Per bag:** 2-3 oz (a generous handful)
- **One 7.5 oz bag fills:** about 3 party favor portions
- **For 20 favors:** 7 bags
- **For 30 favors:** 10 bags

### Gift Bags and Stocking Stuffers
- **One 7.5 oz bag** is the perfect size for a single gift — it feels generous without being excessive
- **For bulk gifting** (teachers, coworkers, clients): buy the 10-pack and save on cost per bag

## Party Ideas by Occasion

### Kids' Birthday Parties
Gummy bears are a birthday party staple, and they work for every age. Here are a few ideas:

- **Candy bar station:** Set out gummy bears alongside other candies, small cups, and scoops. Let kids fill their own bags.
- **Cupcake toppers:** Press a few gummy bears on top of frosted cupcakes. Simple and kids love it.
- **Trail mix bags:** Mix gummy bears with pretzels, popcorn, and chocolate chips for a sweet-salty combo.
- **Color sorting game:** Give each kid a cup of gummy bears and have them sort by color. First one done wins a prize (more gummy bears, obviously).

**Tip for parents:** If other parents are conscious about artificial dyes — and more are every year — choose dye-free gummy bears so you don't have to worry about ingredient questions.

### 4th of July and Patriotic Events
Red, yellow, orange, green, and watermelon gummy bears make for a colorful spread at any patriotic celebration.

- **Flag-themed display:** Arrange gummy bears by color in a flag pattern on a white tray
- **Mason jar favors:** Layer gummy bears in small mason jars for a classic Americana look
- **Add to a charcuterie board:** Gummy bears on a dessert charcuterie board with crackers, chocolate, and dried fruit

### Weddings and Showers
Gummy bears as wedding favors have gotten increasingly popular — they're affordable, universally liked, and easy to package.

- **Clear favor bags with ribbon:** Simple, elegant, cost-effective
- **Custom labels:** Add a sticker with the couple's names and date
- **Candy buffet table:** Gummy bears in apothecary jars alongside other sweets

### Office and Corporate Events
- **Desk candy bowls:** A 7.5 oz bag lasts about a week in a shared desk bowl
- **Meeting room snacks:** Pour into small dishes at conference tables
- **Client gifts:** Include a bag in gift baskets or welcome packages

### Holiday Gift Bags
Gummy bears work for every holiday:
- **Halloween:** Add to trick-or-treat hand-outs (sealed bags are preferred by parents)
- **Christmas/Hanukkah:** Stocking stuffers or gift bag additions
- **Easter:** Tuck into Easter baskets alongside other treats
- **Valentine's Day:** Small bags with a note for classmates

## Choosing Gummy Bears for Groups

When you're buying candy for a group — especially one with kids — you need to think about a few things:

### Dietary Concerns
- **Artificial dyes:** Increasingly a concern for parents. More schools and families are going dye-free. Choosing dye-free gummy bears eliminates this issue entirely.
- **Gelatin:** Standard gummy bears contain gelatin (animal-derived). This matters for vegetarian/vegan guests. If this is a concern, look for pectin-based alternatives.
- **Allergens:** Most gummy bears are free of the top 8 allergens (peanuts, tree nuts, dairy, eggs, wheat, soy, fish, shellfish), but always check the label for facility cross-contamination warnings.
- **Gluten:** Traditional gummy bears are gluten-free, but some brands add wheat starch. Check the label.

### Quality Signals
Not all gummy bears are created equal. For events where the candy represents you — client gifts, wedding favors, party tables — look for:
- **Made in USA** for quality and safety assurance
- **No artificial dyes** (cleaner ingredient list, better for kids)
- **Sealed, branded packaging** (looks intentional, not like you scooped from a bulk bin)
