# Made-in-USA Substantiation — Public-Source Baseline

**Status:** CANONICAL (decision-grade baseline; revise on private supplier audit)
**Version:** 1.0 — 2026-04-29
**Owner:** Ben (founder-only audit; not Drew, not delegated)
**Triggered by:** America 250 trademark pivot (4/29) — opens the broader question of how we substantiate "Made in USA" without the licensing umbrella.
**Authority:** FTC 2021 Made-in-USA Labeling Rule, 16 CFR Part 323 — "all or virtually all" standard.

---

## Why this exists

We dropped the America 250 license, so the marketing copy is now non-trademarked ("America's 250th"). That removes one risk vector but exposes a separate one: **every "Made in USA" claim on the bag, the website, and Amazon listings is gated by 16 CFR 323.** Unqualified "Made in USA" requires *all or virtually all* of the product (final assembly + significant processing + ingredients/components) to be of US origin. Civil penalty: up to $51,744 per violation (2024 adjustment).

Ben asked: **"Can we find this info publicly?"** — i.e., can we audit our own bag-of-materials using only public-source research, without contacting Powers Confections sales or pulling supplier C-of-As? Answer: **partially yes, partially no.** This doc captures the public-source baseline so Ben can decide today which claims to use and what's left to ask Powers privately.

---

## Verdict (one paragraph)

**Unqualified "Made in USA" is NOT defensible** for our 7.5 oz bag at this time. Citric acid, malic acid, pectin, carnauba wax, and most natural colors (annatto, paprika, purple/black carrot, turmeric) are *structurally* non-domestic — there is no merchant US producer for several of these as of 2026. Any single one of them in our formula breaks the "all or virtually all" threshold.

**What IS defensible right now**, with no private audit needed:
1. **Tier 1 (factory-of-origin claims, outside 16 CFR 323):** "Manufactured in Spokane, Washington" / "Crafted by Powers Confections" / "Packaged in Wisconsin."
2. **Tier 2 (qualified MUSA, inside 16 CFR 323 but compliant):** "Made in USA from domestic and imported ingredients."

**Tier 3 (DO NOT use without a completed supplier audit proving "all or virtually all"):** "Made in USA," "100% American-made," "American Made," US-flag imagery + "USA" prominently with no qualifier (FTC treats this as an implied unqualified claim).

## Tiered label language Ben can use today

### Tier 1 — Use freely, no FTC risk

These are factory-of-origin / processing-location claims and fall **outside** 16 CFR 323's scope.

- "Manufactured in Spokane, Washington"
- "Crafted by Powers Confections, Spokane, WA"
- "Packaged in Wisconsin"
- "USA-Packaged"
- "Proudly made by an American family business"
- "Distributed by USA Gummies, Wyoming"

### Tier 2 — Qualified MUSA, defensible

Compliant under 16 CFR 323.1(a)(2): qualifier must be **clear, conspicuous, and adjacent** to the claim — same font size, same panel, no asterisk-buried-in-fine-print.

- "Made in USA from domestic and imported ingredients"
- "Manufactured in the USA with globally sourced ingredients"
- "Produced in the USA from US and imported components"

### Tier 3 — DO NOT USE without a completed supplier audit

These trigger 16 CFR 323 and the 2021 Rule's civil penalties:

- "Made in USA" (unqualified)
- "100% American-Made"
- "American Made"
- US flag imagery + "USA" prominently displayed without an adjacent qualifier (FTC treats flag+text as an *implied* unqualified MUSA claim)

## Recent FTC enforcement (small-CPG analogs)

- **Lions Not Sheep (FTC, 2022)** — apparel brand, $211,335 penalty for "Made in USA" labels on imported garments with US tags swapped in. Closest small-brand analog: founder-led, e-commerce-heavy, used USA imagery aggressively. (FTC Matter No. 2123032.)
- **Williams-Sonoma (2024, $3.17M)** and **Kubota North America (2024, $2M)** — both for unqualified MUSA on products with imported components. Williams-Sonoma matters because the FTC has signaled food/CPG is in scope, not just apparel/equipment.

## Decision rule going forward

1. **Default the bag and storefront to Tier 1 + Tier 2 simultaneously**: "Manufactured in Spokane, WA · Packaged in Wisconsin" plus "Made in USA from domestic and imported ingredients."
2. **Strip Tier 3 phrasing** ("Made in USA," "100% American-made") from anywhere it currently appears in copy until Ben completes the Powers audit. Flag-only imagery is fine; flag + "USA" prominent text without a qualifier is not.
3. **After Powers audit:** if every ingredient resolves US-origin, we can graduate to Tier 3 ("Made in USA") with documented substantiation in this file. If even one structurally-imported ingredient remains (citric acid is the prime suspect — almost certain to disqualify us), we stay at Tier 2 indefinitely.
4. **Revisit annually.** Supplier sourcing changes; re-run the audit each year.
