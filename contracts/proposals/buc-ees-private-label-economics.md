## 3. Regulatory tailwind — VERIFIED FACTS (corrects Ben's Slack sketch)

> **CRITICAL CORRECTION:** Ben's pushback message in `#wholesale` 2026-04-30 09:56 PT mentioned "CA AB 418 already bans Red 40 / Yellow 5 / Blue 1 / Blue 2." **This is factually wrong** and would be a Spottswood-pattern leak risk if used verbatim with Charmaine. The actual bills are below. Verified via WebSearch 2026-04-30.

### 3.1 California — what's actually banned, when

| Bill | Effective | Scope | Dyes/additives banned |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CA AB 418** | Jan 1, 2027 | **Statewide** (all foods sold in CA) | Red 3, brominated vegetable oil, potassium bromate, propylparaben |
| **CA AB 2316** | Dec 31, 2027 | **CA SCHOOL FOODS only** (not statewide retail) | Blue 1, Blue 2, Green 3, Red 40, Yellow 5, Yellow 6 |

**The honest framing:** CA does not yet ban Red 40 / Yellow 5 / Blue 1 / Blue 2 statewide. AB 2316 only restricts them from being served in CA public schools (breakfast, lunch, competitive foods during school hours). Albanese / Haribo can still sell those dyes in California retail.

### 3.2 Texas — the bill that actually matters for Buc-ee's

**Texas SB 25** (NOT SB 1545 — Ben had the wrong number). Signed by Governor Abbott 2025. **Effective January 1, 2027.**

- Applies to packaged foods + beverages sold in Texas containing any of 44 specific dyes/additives, including Red 40, Yellow 5, Yellow 6, Blue 1, Blue 2, Green 3, titanium dioxide, partially hydrogenated oils, olestra, bleached flour, and 33 others.
- **Does NOT ban these ingredients** — requires a warning label that reads (per published bill text): "WARNING: This product contains an ingredient that is not recommended for human consumption by the appropriate authority in Australia, Canada, the European Union, or the United Kingdom."
- **Penalty:** up to $50,000 per product per day plus state legal costs.

**Why this is the angle for Buc-ee's specifically:**
- Buc-ee's HQ is Texas. Most stores are in Texas. SB 25 applies to every shelf in their flagship state.
- By Jan 1, 2027 (≈8 months from a typical category-buyer planning horizon), **every commercial gummy bear with Red 40/Yellow 5 on Buc-ee's Texas shelves needs a warning label** comparing the product to ingredients flagged by Australia, Canada, EU, UK regulators.
- USA Gummies is already dye-free → zero warning label, zero packaging revision, zero $50K/day exposure.
- This is a real, defensible, factually accurate competitive angle. It is **not** a "the dye is banned" claim — it is a "your incumbent suppliers are going to need an unflattering warning sticker on their package in your home state" claim.

### 3.3 Federal context (worth one line)

HHS + FDA announced in 2025 they would phase out petroleum-based synthetic dyes from the food supply (timeline soft). State-by-state action is moving faster than federal. The directional momentum is clear: dye-using brands face an expanding compliance overhead curve; dye-free brands don't.

## 5. Private-label scoping (Charmaine's actual question)

Charmaine asked: *"are you interested in private labeling or only in the USA Gummies brand?"*

Honest answer: we are open to it, but the economics break down differently than Charmaine probably expects, because our COGS is dominated by per-bag manufacturing — not by labeling. A private-label run does not lower COGS meaningfully unless Powers gives us a volume break.

### 5.1 What changes under private label

| Layer | USA Gummies brand | Private label (Buc-ee's brand) | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Powers manufacturing | $1.52/bag | $1.52/bag (same gummy) | $0 — unless Powers offers volume break |
| Belmark primary film | Existing branded SKU | **Custom film + plates one-time charge** | `[needs Belmark quote]` — typical $3K–$8K plate setup + per-roll cost similar |
| UPC | Existing GS1 prefix | **Buc-ee's provides their own UPC** | $0 to us |
| Custom artwork | Existing | **Custom design** — we can do, or Buc-ee's design team provides | $0–$2K depending on who designs |
| Uline secondary | $0.25/bag | $0.25/bag (same MC + cases) | $0 |
| Co-pack run at Powers | Existing schedule | **New SKU = new co-pack run, MOQ likely 25K+ bags** | Capital + lead time |
| **Operating COGS at scale** | **$1.79** | **~$1.79 + setup amortization** | **Nominal — probably +$0.05–$0.15/bag depending on commit volume** |

### 5.2 Why private label is NOT a magic price-drop lever

Charmaine's commodity-vs-USA-Gummies framing assumes private label could deliver something close to her $0.19/oz target. **It cannot, because Powers doesn't make Albanese-grade commodity gummies.** Powers is a smaller batch confectioner with cleaner ingredient sourcing — that's exactly *why* we use them and why we can claim dye-free + made-in-USA. A private-label Buc-ee's branded gummy at Powers would have the same COGS structure as our branded SKU, plus setup costs.

**The honest pitch to Charmaine on private label:** "Yes, we can do a Buc-ee's-branded version. The economics are the same as our branded SKU because the cost is in the gummy, not the bag. The reason to do private label is if you want a **Texas-themed pack design + Buc-ee's UPC** for shelf differentiation — not to hit Albanese commodity pricing. We'd need a 25K+ bag commit to justify the custom film + plate setup."

### 5.3 What we'd need before quoting a private-label MOQ

- **Powers volume-tier pricing** (see §2.5 — Ben direct to Greg Kroetch).
- **Belmark custom-film plate quote** + per-roll cost at 25K, 50K, 100K bag commits. **Ben action (Belmark Link rep).**
- **Buc-ee's order velocity expectation.** A 50K-bag private-label commit is 1 truckload. Is that 6 weeks of velocity for Buc-ee's, 6 months, or 6 quarters? Charmaine has the data; we don't.
- **Net 60 vs Net 30 working-capital impact** at private-label volumes.
