# USA Gummies Sustainability Snippet — canonical copy

The story is **a 100%-domestic supply chain**, not recycled materials.
Every claim below is verifiable from the supply chain itself: the
gummy candy, the flavor, the colors, and the plastic film of the bag
are all sourced and produced inside the United States. Inventory only
moves between domestic facilities — no overseas freight, no
transcontinental cargo, no air-freight legs.

What we **do not** claim:
- Recycled materials of any kind
- Compostable / biodegradable packaging
- Carbon neutral / net zero
- Specific carbon-reduction percentages (no LCA study to back them)
- Any vendor or facility name

What we **do** claim, in order of strongest defense:
1. Every component of the bag — the candy, the colors, the flavor,
   the film — is made in the U.S.A.
2. The entire supply chain operates inside the United States.
   Inventory moves between domestic facilities only.
3. No overseas shipping legs. No transcontinental cargo. No
   air-freight on raw materials or finished goods.
4. Domestic-only freight means a shorter route per bag than imports
   whose ingredients crossed an ocean before reaching the consumer.
